Solar-Powered Outdoor Oven

Materials Required:

  • Cardboard or wood panels
  • Reflective material (aluminum foil)
  • Glass or transparent plastic sheet
  • Insulation (e.g., foam board)
  • Adhesive (e.g., glue, tape)
  • Black paint or heat-resistant paint
  • Cooking rack or grill
  • Thermometer

Procedure:

  1. Construct a box frame using cardboard or wood panels to create the oven structure.
  2. Cover the interior of the box with reflective material (aluminum foil) to maximize sunlight absorption.
  3. Add insulation around the sides and bottom of the box to retain heat.
  4. Create a transparent lid using glass or transparent plastic sheet to trap heat inside the oven.
  5. Paint the exterior of the box with black paint or heat-resistant paint to absorb more sunlight.
  6. Install a cooking rack or grill inside the oven for placing food.
  7. Place a thermometer inside the oven to monitor the temperature.
  8. Position the solar oven in direct sunlight and adjust its angle throughout the day to capture maximum sunlight.
  9. Once the oven reaches the desired temperature, place food on the cooking rack and cook as usual.
  10. Monitor the temperature periodically and adjust the position of the oven as needed to maintain heat.
